AI Engineer Roadmap: Zero to Hired in 6 Months
Who this is for: Students with no prior coding experience who want to become job-ready AI Engineers - not researchers, not ML PhDs. The people building real LLM-powered products that companies actually pay for.
Time commitment: ~15-20 hours/week for 6 months. Less, and you'll stretch this to 9-12 months. More, and you can compress it to 4.
The philosophy:
? Engineering first, then AI on top
? Build something every single week (even if it's small)
? Evaluation is the #1 skill that separates AI engineers from "API wrappers"
? 3 portfolio projects on GitHub > any certificate

The 3 Mistakes That Kill Most Students
1. Watching tutorials without building. If you finish a week and don't have a project on GitHub, you didn't do that week.
2. Skipping evaluation. Anyone can call an API. People who can prove their AI system actually works get hired.
3. Building generic projects. "I made a chatbot" gets you 0 interviews. "I built a RAG system that helps lawyers find precedents in 200ms with 92% retrieval accuracy" gets you 5.
